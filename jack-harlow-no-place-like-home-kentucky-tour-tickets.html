    Jack Harlow might be a superstar but he hasn’t lost sight of where he came from, and he’s proved this by expanding his yearly hometown show into a tour of the state.

    On Thursday (August 17), the Kentucky rapper announced that he will be performing six shows across the Southern region that raised him. The No Place Like Home tour will run for a week and a half this winter — it is scheduled to commence in late November and run into early December.

    AD LOADING...

    Advanced registration for tickets is open until Sunday (August 20), followed by a limited number of general admission tickets which go on sale on Friday (August 25). Get your passes here.

    Despite being in high demand, Harlow occasioanlly joins the audience rather than always being the one on stage. Always putting his family first, the 25-year-old MC had the pleasure of introducing his mother to Beyoncé when her Renaissance Tour recently rolled into his hometown.

    The “First Class” rapper was able to introduce his mom, Maggie Harlow, to Bey backstage before her show in Louisville, Kentucky in mid-July. Soon after, a photo of the moment went public, showing the two women engaged in conversation as Harlow watched on.

    AD LOADING...

    Beyoncé was even seen holding a tall gift bag — the kind usually used to carry a bottle of liquor or spirits – although it’s unclear if she was gifted this from Jack and his mom.

    “Introduced my Mom to Beyoncé in Louisville, Kentucky tonight,” Harlow wrote on Threads.
