    Paula Yates Age, Biography

    Paula Yates was a British television presenter and writer who was born to English parents on April 24, 1959, in Colwyn Bay, Wales. When it comes to nationality, she is British, and her ethnicity is British-White. Elaine Smith, her mother, was a former showgirl, actress, and erotic novelist who went by the stage name Heller Toren. Yates grew up in a showbiz family and thought her biological father was Jess Yates, who hosted the ITV religious program Stars on Sunday.

    In that same year, a DNA test revealed that her biological father was actually game show host Hughie Green. Despite growing up in an entertainment family, Yates described her childhood as lonely and isolated. Yates’ parents’ marriage was unconventional, and after their divorce in 1975, she spent most of her time with her mother, despite having a closer relationship with her father. Linda Green, Christopher Green, and Barry Green were Yates’ three siblings. On April 24, 2022, she celebrated her 41st birthday for the final time.

    Before returning to the United Kingdom, Paula attended several schools in Malta and Mallorca, including Penrhos College, Ysgol Aberconwy, and Bellver International College.

    Paula Yates Husband

    Paula Yates was a married woman who had only been married once in her life. She married Bob Geldof on August 31, 1986, in Las Vegas. She dated Bob Geldof after meeting him in the early days of the Boomtown Rat. In May 1996, Geldof and Yates divorced.

    Fifi Trixibelle Geldof, Peaches Geldof, and Pixie Geldof are the children of Paula Yates. With her ex-husband, Bob Geldof, she had three children. Fifi, their first child, was born in 1983, followed by Peaches on March 13, 1989, and Pixie on September 17, 1990. She also had a child with her boyfriend and singer Michael Hutchence, Heavenly Haraani Tiger Lily.

    Paula Yates’s Net Worth

    Cause Of Death

    Paula Yates died on September 17, 2000, at the age of 41, from a heroin overdose, on the occasion of her daughter Pixie’s tenth birthday. Despite the fact that the coroner ruled it was not a suicide, but rather the result of “foolish and incautious” behavior. Yates was discovered with her four-year-old daughter Tiger Lily. It was later revealed that she had not used illegal drugs in nearly two years prior to her death.
