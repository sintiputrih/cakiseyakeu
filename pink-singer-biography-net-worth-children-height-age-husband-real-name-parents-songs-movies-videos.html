    Biography

    Pink (born September 8, 1979), an esteemed artist known for her multifaceted talents, is Alecia Beth Moore Hart, a versatile American actress, singer, and songwriter.

    P!nk was a vital part of the girl group Choice during her formative years. Her solo debut album, Can’t Take Me Home, released in 2000, achieved double-platinum status in the United States. This remarkable achievement was fueled by R&B influences and the chart-topping singles There You Go and Most Girls on the Billboard Hot 100.

    Furthermore, Pink‘s global recognition soared with the collaborative hit Lady Marmalade from the Moulin Rouge! soundtrack, topping charts worldwide and solidifying her place in the music industry.

    Early Life & Education

    Alecia Beth Moore, renowned as P!nk, was born on September 8, 1979, in Doylestown, Pennsylvania. She hailed from a family with a deep appreciation for music and discovered her vocal prowess at a tender age.

    However, her childhood was marked by her parents’ separation—Jim Moore and Judith Moore—which prompted her to rebel against authority figures. She shared her upbringing with her brother, Jason Moore.

    In her quest for knowledge, Pink attended institutions like Moore College of Art and Design, Central Bucks High School West, and Lenape Middle School. Despite facing financial challenges, her passion for music remained unwavering. 

    P!nk‘s breakthrough came with Lady Marmalade, and she became celebrated for her unique voice, rebellious spirit, and empowering lyrics. With over 90 million records sold globally, numerous awards, and a commitment to philanthropy and social activism, P!nk is a luminary in the music industry and a testament to resilience and compassion.

    Career

    Pink‘s career post-2008 witnessed a remarkable transformation. Her album Funhouse (2008) showcased her resilience and empowerment with hits like So What. She also ventured into social commentary with Dear Mr. President.

    In 2010, The Truth About Love introduced a blend of rock and pop with the catchy Blow Me (One Last Kiss).

    P!nk‘s versatility extended to film, voicing Gloria in Happy Feet Two and acting in Thanks for Sharing. Her 2017 album Beautiful Trauma, offered introspection with the title track.

    Her iconic Super Bowl LII performance in 2018 left an indelible mark on the musical world. Pink‘s unwavering dedication to pushing boundaries and crafting soulful melodies remains unparalleled.

    Personal Life

    Pink‘s romantic history is a tale of relationships with Todd Morse and Colt Prattes, along with brief encounters with Tommy Lee (2003), Kristanna Loken (2003), and Joey Fatone (2000). Rumors have also linked her to Anthony ‘Treach’ Criss.

    Pink and Carey Hart have been married for over 17 years since January 7, 2006. They share two children: a daughter named Willow Sage and a son, Jameson Moon. Their unique love story began at the Philadelphia X Games in 2001, with a brief breakup in 2003. Pink proposed to Hart during a motocross race in Mammoth Lakes in June 2005, an unconventional and unforgettable moment that sealed their commitment.

    Pink‘s accumulated net worth is estimated to be around $200 million. Her success in the music industry can be attributed to her powerful vocals and numerous chart-topping hits such as Raise Your Glass, F**kin Perfect, Blow Me (One Last Kiss), and Try.
