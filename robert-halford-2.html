    Mitt Romney Biography

    Mitt Romney was born in 1947 as one of four children to Lenore Romney and George W. Romney. His birthplace was Detroit, which is located in Michigan. His father was a car executive, and his mother was a housewife. He is primarily of English descent but also has German and Scottish ancestors.

    Jane, Margo, and Scott are his three older siblings. His parents named him after a family friend, an entrepreneur named J. Willard Marriott, and his father’s cousin, Milton “Mitt” Romney, a former Chicago Bears quarterback.

    Career

    Mitt Romney began his career as a Mormon missionary in France for two and a half years beginning in 1966. By 1971, he was involved in both of his parents’ political movements.

    Furthermore, in 1971, he received a BA from Brigham Young University, and in 1975, he received a joint JD–MBA from Harvard University. This politician became a management consultant and was hired by Bain & Company in 1977.

    As CEO of Bain, he was primarily responsible for moving the company ahead of a financial crisis. Romney co-founded and ran the spin-off firm named Bain Capital in 1984, which was known as a highly commercial private equity-type investment organization that became one of the types in the country.

    Following his departure from Bain Capital and his native leadership role within the LDS Church. He ran for the United States Senate as a Republican in the 1994 Massachusetts election. Furthermore, after defeating long-time incumbent Ted Kennedy, he regains his position at Bain Capital.
