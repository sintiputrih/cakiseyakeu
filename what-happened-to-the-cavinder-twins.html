    What Happened to The Cavinder Twins?

    The Cavinder twins, Hanna and Haley, have made headlines for their remarkable accomplishments both on and off the basketball court. From their early success in high school to their collegiate basketball journey, the twins have left an indelible mark in the world of sports and social media.

    18 Jun 2024, 00:00

    The Cavinder twins, Hanna and Haley, have made headlines for their remarkable accomplishments both on and off the basketball court.

    From their early success in high school to their collegiate basketball journey, the twins have left an indelible mark in the world of sports and social media.

    Early Success in Basketball

    Hanna and Haley's basketball careers began at Gilbert High School in Arizona, where they showcased their prowess on the court, with Hanna being honored as the Arizona 5A Offensive Player of the Year.

    Collegiate Basketball Journey

    Transitioning to collegiate basketball, the Cavinder twins made waves at Fresno State, quickly becoming standout players. Hanna earned recognition as a two-time All-Mountain West (MW) Conference selection and a spot on the MW All-Freshman Team.

    Meanwhile, Haley garnered attention as an All-MW selection in each of her three seasons at Fresno State, culminating in her being named the MW Player of the Year during her sophomore season.

    They continued to excel on the court as members of the Miami Hurricanes basketball team, contributing to the team's success and an Elite 8 appearance in the NCAA Tournament.

    Despite their success at Miami, Haley announced that she will play one final season at Texas Christian University (TCU) in 2024-25, while Hanna moved to conclude her basketball career and is now focused on social media and brand management efforts.

    Social Media Influence

    The Cavinder twins are celebrated for their influential presence on social media platforms. Proclaimed as the Queens of NIL, they were the very first college athletes to sign an NIL deal on July 1, 2021.

    With millions of followers across platforms like Snapchat, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube, they've established themselves as leading figures in the realm of social media.

    They have used their platform to discuss the college-athlete pay debate, attending Sen.

    Ted Cruz's round-table in defense of athletes' rights to profit off their name, image, and likeness, and have benefited tremendously from social media, earning nearly $2M from NIL and brand deals.

    Transition from Basketball to Business

    Despite their success in basketball, the twins have decided to focus on their business ventures.

    Hanna Cavinder has chosen to focus on business, while Haley has elected to play her fifth and final season at TCU.

    They have emphasized the importance of investing their earnings and building a long-term brand and business, which they attribute to the connections and relationships built through NIL.

    Impact on the College Athletics Landscape

    The Cavinder twins' journey has not only transformed their lives but has also influenced the college athletics landscape, particularly in the context of NIL regulations.

    They have been vocal advocates for student-athletes' rights and have leveraged their experiences to inspire and educate others about the potential of NIL opportunities in college sports.

    On the whole, the Cavinder twins have seamlessly transitioned from college basketball stars to influential social media figures.

    Their journey serves as a testament to the evolving landscape of college athletics, particularly in the context of NIL regulations.

    As they continue to inspire future generations of athletes and content creators, their impact is sure to endure, leaving a lasting legacy in both the worlds of sports and social media.
