    Zuri Adele is an American actress who came into prominence after playing Michelle in the short film Russian Roulette (2010). She went on to gain critical acclaim for her role as Malika Williams on the Freeform television comedy-drama Good Trouble, having debuted in its pilot episode in 2019. Adele has been known for her work with several leading theatres like the True Colors Theatre in Atlanta and the Tony Award-winning Alliance Theatre. Zuri has taught acting workshops at the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls in South Africa, and endeavored to provide opportunities for students to network as well as study internationally. She is the recipient of many honors and accolades such as the Roger Bobb Scholarship that was awarded to her by The Black Women Film Network in 2012. Zuri has amassed more than 60k followers on Instagram.

    Zuri attended Gunn High School in Palo Alto, graduating in 2008 being a winner of the Gunn High School Faculty Cup. She then went to Spelman College in Atlanta and acquired her BA degree in Drama.
